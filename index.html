Platform Architecture
Design Philosophy
Horizontal trust layer — not a vertical tool

Narratio is designed as infrastructure, not as an application. It integrates into existing enterprise stacks via API without requiring migrations, tooling replacement, or changes to existing workflows.

The platform ingests heterogeneous data, normalises it, seals provenance, executes verification flows, and produces structured evidence — all through a single, composable pipeline that works the same way regardless of sector or use case.

Deployment Model
On-premise GPU infrastructure + API-first delivery

Narratio runs on dedicated GPU infrastructure (NVIDIA L40s cluster) hosted on-premise. This is a deliberate architectural decision: enterprise clients in regulated sectors require data sovereignty, predictable latency, and independence from third-party cloud AI providers.

The platform is delivered as a managed API. Clients integrate via REST endpoints — no SDKs, no proprietary agents, no lock-in. Integration depth scales with usage, not with engineering effort.

Why on-prem GPU? Enterprise clients in energy, industrial, and public sectors require data sovereignty guarantees that cloud-based AI providers cannot contractually offer. On-prem infrastructure also eliminates per-token cost exposure at scale — VARS Polska's 270,000 flows/month would be economically unviable on third-party LLM APIs. The infrastructure investment (€80K–€120K depending on scenario) replaces what would be €300K+/year in cloud AI costs at current volume.
The Structural Problem
Every organization operates on information it cannot structurally defend.

This is not a technology gap. It is a structural problem. Organizations across every regulated sector — energy, banking, pharma, infrastructure, media, public administration — rely on data and content for decisions, reporting, and public communication. But when challenged by regulators, auditors, or public scrutiny, they cannot demonstrate how that information was built, which sources were used, or why the output should be trusted.

The manual processes used to validate information do not scale. The governance tools that manage data do not explain it. The AI systems that generate content do not make it auditable. No one is solving the last mile: turning information into defensible evidence.

Narratio exists to solve this structural gap. We are building the future of traceability, security, and data sovereignty — not as a feature within existing tools, but as a new category of infrastructure that organizations will require as a structural dependency.

Building the Future of Traceability, Security & Data Sovereignty
Traceability
From data governance to informational provenance

Current data governance tools (Collibra, Alation, Informatica) manage metadata, policies, and data lineage at a structural level. But they do not operate at the level of claims, content, or explainable verification — they cannot tell you why a specific KPI, report, or publication should be trusted.

Narratio operates downstream of governance — taking governed data as input and producing traceable, explainable, auditable evidence with a structured TrustScore and Evidence Graph. This is the missing layer between "data exists" and "data is defensible."

Security & Sovereignty
On-premise infrastructure — data never leaves the perimeter

Narratio runs on dedicated GPU infrastructure (NVIDIA L40s cluster) hosted on-premise. Enterprise clients in regulated sectors require data sovereignty, predictable latency, and independence from third-party cloud AI providers. Unlike cloud-based AI tools, Narratio contractually guarantees that no data is processed outside the client's jurisdictional boundary.

This is not a deployment preference — it is a structural prerequisite for regulated enterprise deployment. EU AI Act, GDPR, DSA, and sector-specific regulation make data sovereignty a contractual requirement, not a feature request.

Defensibility
Evidence Graph as structural moat

Every verification, every traced dataset, every provenance seal generates a node in Narratio's Evidence Graph. This graph grows with every client interaction, becoming richer and more defensible over time.

Switching cost is not contractual — it is structural. The deeper the integration, the more workflows depend on Narratio's traceability outputs. This creates operational dependency, not vendor lock-in. The graph cannot be replicated by a competitor because it is built through usage.

Integration Model
Horizontal layer — embeds into existing stacks via API

Narratio integrates into CMS, BI tools, data pipelines, and operational platforms — without migrations, tooling replacement, or workflow changes. One API call returns a TrustScore, Evidence Graph, and audit-ready metadata.

The platform sits downstream of data governance and observability, and upstream of decision-making and publication. Integration depth scales with usage, not with engineering effort.

Go To Market — Attacking the Structural Gap
We do not sell productivity improvements. We sell defensibility when organizations must prove how their information was built.

Narratio's GTM is built around a structural thesis: traceability, security, and data sovereignty are becoming non-negotiable infrastructure for any organization that operates under regulatory, reputational, or operational scrutiny. EU AI Act, Digital Services Act, CSRD/ESRS, and sector-specific regulation are creating systemic demand for provenance, explainability, and auditability — and no existing tool category covers the full stack.

We enter through the risk context of the prospect — not through product features. The signal that activates a commercial process is always external: regulatory pressure, public exposure, audit failure, reputational crisis, or systemic inability to defend published information. Narratio does not compete on "process optimization." It competes when the cost of not being able to explain information exceeds the cost of the platform.

GTM Philosophy

Signal-driven, not volume-driven. Sales are activated only when objective urgency exists. No cold outbound without a confirmed market signal.

Enterprise-first, top-down. Entry via CDO, VP Data, Head of Compliance, CISO, or equivalent decision-makers with budget authority over data governance, compliance, or operational infrastructure.

Small pipeline, high qualification. Pipeline is managed as a system, not as isolated opportunities. Only accounts exceeding a ≥60% activation threshold enter the commercial pipeline.

Land & Expand. Initial entry targets one critical workflow. Expansion is driven by usage volume, additional datasets, and module adoption — not by upselling seats.

Pipeline Stages

1. Observation — Market monitoring, signal detection, regulatory and public exposure tracking.

2. Authority Activation — Targeted outreach through problem framing. Entry via the prospect's risk context.

3. Active Pipeline — Commercial process open, ICP-qualified, buyer identified, proposal stage.

4. Proof of Value — Paid, scope-defined PoV with explicit success criteria.

5. License & Recurring Usage — Production deployment, recurring revenue, expansion through usage.

This model scales revenue density per account rather than logo count — enabling predictable ARR growth with controlled sales complexity.

ICP — Ideal Customer Profile
Organizations that cannot structurally defend their information

An organization qualifies as ICP when at least three of the following conditions are present: information is used for critical operational, strategic, or public decisions; data or claims are publicly exposed or auditable; regulatory, ESG, compliance, or sensitive reporting obligations exist; manual or consultative validation processes have proven non-scalable.

The common thread across all target accounts is the same structural problem: they operate on information they cannot defend. Whether it is a bank that cannot trace how a regulatory report was built, a pharma company that cannot explain the sourcing of a clinical claim, or a media group that cannot verify audience data before publication — the inability to demonstrate informational provenance is a systemic liability.
