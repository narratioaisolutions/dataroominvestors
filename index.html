Platform Architecture
Design Philosophy
Horizontal trust layer — not a vertical tool

Narratio is designed as infrastructure, not as an application. It integrates into existing enterprise stacks via API without requiring migrations, tooling replacement, or changes to existing workflows.

The platform ingests heterogeneous data, normalises it, seals provenance, executes verification flows, and produces structured evidence — all through a single, composable pipeline that works the same way regardless of sector or use case.

Deployment Model
On-premise GPU infrastructure + API-first delivery

Narratio runs on dedicated GPU infrastructure (NVIDIA L40s cluster) hosted on-premise. This is a deliberate architectural decision: enterprise clients in regulated sectors require data sovereignty, predictable latency, and independence from third-party cloud AI providers.

The platform is delivered as a managed API. Clients integrate via REST endpoints — no SDKs, no proprietary agents, no lock-in. Integration depth scales with usage, not with engineering effort.

Architecture Principles
◇
Data Sovereignty
No data leaves the infrastructure. GPU on-prem ensures full control for regulated clients.
◈
API-First
Every capability exposed via REST. No SDKs, no agents, no lock-in. Composable by design.
▣
Audit by Default
Every operation is logged, timestamped, and traceable. The platform audits itself as it audits data.
◉
Explainability
No black boxes. Every TrustScore is decomposable into its evidence components and source chain.
Why on-prem GPU? Enterprise clients in energy, industrial, and public sectors require data sovereignty guarantees that cloud-based AI providers cannot contractually offer. On-prem infrastructure also eliminates per-token cost exposure at scale — VARS Polska's 270,000 flows/month would be economically unviable on third-party LLM APIs. The infrastructure investment (€80K–€120K depending on scenario) replaces what would be €300K+/year in cloud AI costs at current volume.
Mission & Vision
Mission
To make information defensible — by turning data into structured, traceable, and explainable evidence that organizations can stand behind.
Narratio exists because the inability to explain how information was built is becoming a systemic liability. Regulatory pressure, public scrutiny, and reputational risk have created a structural gap between how organizations produce information and how they must defend it. We close that gap.
Vision
To become the standard trust infrastructure layer for enterprise information — the same way AWS became the standard for compute and Okta for identity.
Narratio is not a vertical tool. It is a horizontal layer that integrates into any stack, any workflow, any sector. Every organization that produces, uses, or publishes information it must defend is a potential user. The category does not yet have an infrastructure owner — we intend to be it.
Strategic Thesis
"The cost of not being able to explain information is becoming higher than the cost of the platform."
Narratio competes when this threshold is crossed. EU AI Act, Digital Services Act, ESG reporting obligations, and the mainstreaming of C2PA content provenance standards are systematically raising this threshold across every sector we target. We do not sell productivity. We sell defensibility — and the market for it is expanding by regulatory mandate.

Key Risks & Mitigations
Risk
Mitigation
High
Revenue Concentration
VARS Polska represents 53% of total ARR (€480K). Loss of this account would significantly impact revenue and growth trajectory.
Mitigation
VARS contract is deeply embedded — 270,000 flows/month across 150 TB of telemetry creates high switching cost. Active pipeline of 7+ accounts targets diversification to <30% concentration by Q4 2026. Land & expand model with Gestamp and Repsol accelerates rebalancing.
High
Founder Dependency
The company operates entirely through two co-founders. No additional engineering, commercial, or operational staff. Key-person risk on both strategic and technical execution.
Mitigation
Primary use of funds includes hiring CTO (Fullstack), Backend Developer, and SDR. Scenario 2 (€500K) doubles engineering capacity. Planned hires are scoped to reduce founder bottleneck within 3–6 months of investment close. Product architecture is documented and modular by design.
Medium
Sales Cycle Length
Enterprise procurement in regulated sectors (energy, aviation, public administration) involves long decision cycles — typically 4–9 months from first contact to signed contract.
Mitigation
Signal-driven GTM model qualifies accounts before outreach — reducing wasted cycles. SaaS subscription track (€2.990/mo) provides a faster-closing revenue stream for mid-market. Pipeline includes accounts at multiple stages to ensure continuous deal flow.
Medium
Category Definition Risk
Trust infrastructure is not yet a recognised enterprise software category. Buyers may struggle to allocate budget or find internal sponsors for a product that doesn't fit existing purchasing categories.
Mitigation
EU AI Act, Digital Services Act, and ESG reporting mandates are creating regulatory tailwinds that force budget allocation. Existing clients validate the category through production usage. Narratio positions against existing budget lines (compliance, data governance, reporting) rather than creating new ones.
Medium
Competitive Emergence
Established players in data governance (Collibra, Informatica) or AI TRiSM could extend into trust verification. Content provenance standards (C2PA) may commoditise parts of the stack.
Mitigation
Narratio operates at the intersection of data governance, content provenance, and AI explainability — none of the existing players cover the full stack. Competitive landscape analysis (available in Document Index) maps positioning across all adjacent categories. Production deployments create defensible moats through integration depth and operational dependency.
Low
Technical Infrastructure Risk
On-prem GPU infrastructure requires maintenance, redundancy planning, and capital investment. Hardware failure could disrupt service delivery.
Mitigation
Use of funds includes redundancy (spares, offsite backup, UPS). Infrastructure is containerised (Docker/K8s), enabling rapid recovery. On-prem model eliminates dependency on cloud provider pricing changes and ensures data sovereignty compliance — a contractual requirement for current enterprise clients.
